Classification & Morphology of Poxviruses

A Comprehensive Academic Review

Presented by: Syrpai Ryngksai

Introduction

  • Largest and most complex human viruses.
  • Only human DNA virus that replicates entirely in the cytoplasm.
  • Family: Poxviridae.
  • Notable for being the first disease eradicated (Smallpox).
  • High medical significance due to re-emergence of zoonotic poxviruses (e.g., Mpox).

Morphology & Structure

Source: Wikimedia Commons

  • Shape: Brick-shaped or ovoid.
  • Size: Large (200-300 nm). Visible under Light Microscope (Paschen bodies).
  • Core: Contains double-stranded DNA.
  • Lateral Bodies: Unique protein structures flanking the core.

Why Cytoplasmic Replication?

Unlike other DNA viruses that require the host nucleus, Poxviruses carry their own machinery:

  • ✓ Carries its own DNA-dependent RNA polymerase.
  • ✓ Carries own transcription factors.
  • ✓ Produces "Inclusion Bodies" (Guarnieri bodies) in the cytoplasm.

Viva Quick-Fire

Q: Largest human virus? A: Poxvirus.

Q: Why do they replicate in the cytoplasm? A: They bring their own enzymes.

Q: What are Guarnieri bodies? A: Cytoplasmic inclusions in Smallpox.
